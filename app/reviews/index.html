KickBeat: Steam Edition

#5

Kickbeat's Steam picture,
as it doesn't have true cover art.


KickBeat has lots of flashing lights
and bright colors, rarer of games today.

KickBeat: Steam Edition is a rhythm game released in 2013 by Zen Studios. It is unique in the fact that it blends music and "fighting" together into a surprisingly fun game. Your goal in KickBeat is to defend the Orb of Music from attackers who want its power. You stand in the center of a circle, and the enemies walk slowly around you. When the reach a side of the circle (left, right, top, or bottom) they will attempt to attack you. When this happens, you have to push the button corresponding to the side they are coming from. (WASD by default.)

Pros Cons
Bright and exciting visuals Can be overwhelming
Good music choices Story is easy to overlook
Action is fun Music is not mapped perfectly
Infinite replayability due to music importing Can feel repetitive
Good variety of preloaded tracks
Verdict = 8.5 out of 10

KickBeat is simple but addictive game. Nothing feels better that whooping random enemies to the beat of your favorite songs, and KickBeat lets you do so near-flawlessly. Its campaign mode has just enough twists to keep it interesting, whether it be a "boss battle" or moving to a new location, with character and enemy outfits to match. While the game can be confusing and even overwhelming (try Master difficulty and you'll understand) it is never frustrating to the point of not being fun anymore. A definite buy for music lovers!


Teleglitch: Die More Edition

#4

Teleglitch's cover art, a throwback
to the days of the Atari 2600.


Teleglitch has very unique visuals,
giving it an interesting feel.

Teleglitch: Die More Edition is an indie top-down shooter released in 2013 by Paradox Interactive. It is very similar to other dungeon-crawler games like Nethack in nature, and does a good job of living up to it. The game features randomly generated levels, healthy amounts of loot, unhealthy amounts of enemies, and plenty of secrets.

Pros Cons
Uniquely interesting graphics VERY steep learning curve
Great level design Story is easy to miss
Combat is fun Objectives not always clear
Amazing atmosphere
Verdict = 8.5 out of 10

Teleglitch is an immersive game, to say the least. The atmosphere that is created through the sparse lighting, strange background noises and strange visual quirks is rare. If I had to compare it, the closest game I've played to this would be the flash game K.O.L.M. Teleglitch has very fun gameplay, involving plenty of exploration, murder, and looting. Overall, I definitely recommend this to anyone looking for an action-horror dungeon-crawling shooter.


Battlefield 4

#3

Battlefield 4's box art,
showcasing the protagonist of its campaign.


The graphics are crazy good,
thanks to the Frostbite 3 engine.

Battlefield 4 is a FPS released by EA and developed by DICE in 2013. It is the sequel to Battlefield 3, and improves on it in many ways. Battlefield 4 focuses on team play unlike some other shooters, and this can make the game more or less fun depending on circumstance. There are tons of maps, many of them being DLC. Vehicles are a good aspect of the game, but can be annoying due to respawn times. Levolution is a mind-blowing aspect of the game, and certainly can make for some interesting battles.

Pros Cons
Good UI and mechanics Buggy release
Well-designed DLCs A few too many DLCs
Maps are well designed Campaign is too short
Great multiplayer experience Battlepacks are stupid
Verdict = 8 out of 10

I love Battlefield 4, but it has its flaws, like every game. First, the game took too long to perfect. Major issues took forever to fix, and even now DICE is still working on perfecting the game. This can make your first impression a negative one, and leave you frustrated while DICE lags behind on bug fixes. Secondly, Battlepacks are one of the dumbest mechanics I've seen is a while. Each battlepack contains random weapons attachments, XP boosts, customization options, or other things. This sounds fine, but there are some attachments that can ONLY be acquired through these, and each attachment is gun-specific. So, you may end up frustrated as you cannot get the attachment you want for the gun you want. And with more than 50 guns in the game, you might be waiting a while. This wouldn't be so bad if they came easily, but you either have to level up for them or BUY them. On the bright side, Battlefield 4 is unique and massively fun, whether you're in a tank, boat, helicopter, or foot soldier. A definite recommendation, especially now that many of the major bugs have been fixed.


Need for Speed Rivals

#2


Rivals' cover art. Quite stylish.



The visuals of this game are stunning,
but looks aren't everything...

Need for Speed Rivals is a racing game released in 2013 by EA. The game was developed by Ghost Games and Criterion, leaving DICE open to work on Battlefield 4. It is similar to NFS Hot Pursuit in style, but has several differences. The world is more rural than Most Wanted's, focusing on straighter roads to complement the top speeds of some of the hypercars. Also, there is more customization than there was in Most Wanted, with car color and performance upgrades being available.

Pros Cons
Good car selection Forgettable story
Actual customization Autolog can be annoying
Multiplayer is good Safehouse system SUCKS
Graphics are beautiful DLCs are expensive and mediocre
Pursuit Tech feels gimmicky
Steep learning curve
Verdict = 6 out of 10

I honestly liked this game, but several things hurt it a LOT. Firstly, the world, while beautifully rendered, is lacking, with few side streets and more dirt paths that you just don't want to drive your Ferrari on. Most roads are fairly straight, and this can cause some boring sections during races. Secondly, the story is TERRIBLE. Most, of the time, you're ignoring it as some guy goes on a monologue over some strange techno visuals. The cars don't feel special, since they are overshadowed by newer, faster ones that you unlock every time you finish a chapter of the game's "story." The DLCs only unlock a couple of cars and are WAY overpriced, at 5 buck for two or three decent cars. On the bright side, the physics are GREAT. Drifting is amazing, and it is pretty easy to pull off some sick moves. Overall, I would recommend to skip Rivals unless it's really your thing, and instead either go for the cheaper Most Wanted, which is better in most ways, or wait for NFS 2015.


Need for Speed Most Wanted (2012)

#1


The box art of the game.



This shows off the amazing detail
and selection of supercars.

Need For Speed Most Wanted (2012) is a racing game released by EA studios and developed by DICE and Criterion Games. It is the spiritual successor to the 2005 Need for Speed Most wanted, but shares many differences. Firstly, do NOT think of this game as the sequel to the original, or you will be disappointed. For sharing the same name, there are very few similarities between the two. This installment focuses more on the sleek and shiny cars than the "gangsta" atmosphere of the first, and the game's interface looks extremely clean to reflect that, as well as the memorable rap music of the first being replaced by below-average pop.

Pros Cons
A TON of cars! (~50) Little customization
Open, very well-designed world Cops are annoying but stupid
Very nice graphics Wants you to buy DLC
If bought, DLCs are very good Not enough races
Verdict: 7.5 out of 10

The game is excellent, but does not live up to its predecessor. Some of its features are great, such as the frantic multiplayer, but it's missing a lot. The "bosses" are way too easy (specially with the DLC that gives you a Bugatti right off the bat), there are only 8 or so events per car, and eventually travelling the beautiful open world becomes pointless thanks to a fast-travel like system. It is an extremely fun game, with high-octane action and crazy stunts, but it can be stress-inducing, especially when driving hypercars that will crash on a pebble. Overall, I recommend this for the fun I've had playing it, but buy at you own risk. If you want to see some gameplay, check out one of my videos here.
